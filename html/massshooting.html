Mass Shooting

There is no widely accepted definition of a mass shooting; some count based on a minimum number of dead victims, or a minimum number of overall victims.

However, the Federal Bureau of Investigation has defined an active shooter incident as one or more individuals “actively engaged in killing or attempting to kill people in a populated area. Implicit in this definition is the shooter’s use of a firearm.” The FBI has not set a minimum number of casualties to qualify as a mass shooting, but U.S. statute (the Investigative Assistance for Violent Crimes Act of 2012) specifies that a “mass killing” is defined as “3 or more killings in a single incident.” For the purposes of this infographic, both sets of criteria are applied to the term "mass shooting," with the distinction that the shooter or shooters are not included in any fatality statistics. The term "spree" is used for shootings involving multiple locations.
